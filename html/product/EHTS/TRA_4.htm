TPE is designed to maintain freeze protection, close temperature tolerances or viscosity control.

It provides an excellent means of maintaining very long, continuous lengths of impulse lines and piping at consistent temperatures end-to-end. TPE should be chosen when steam is not available or when the steam supply could be interrupted such as during shutdowns.

Use TPE if the allowable temperature ranges from 50¢µ(10¡É) to 250¢µ(121¡É). Because it is self regulating, this system will lower its heat output as the material gets warmer. When close temperature control is necessary, TPE can be utilized with an optional line sensing thermostat.		 ¡¡ TPE offers a choice of two standard tracers. A high temperature tracer (XTV) is capable of maintaining temperatures of up to 250¢µ(121¡É) and withstanding blowdown temperatures of 420¢µ(215¡É). A low temperature tracer (BTV) will maintain temperatures up to 150¢µ(65¡É) and withstand process temperatures of up to 185¢µ(85¡É).

Other designs are available to maintain temperatures up to 660¢µ(350¡É) and withstand 1000¢µ(538¡É) blowdown conditions.
Consult factory for specific design.

Electric tracer
Standard TPE-Self Regulating products utilize Chemelex electric tracers. They are FM approved and CSA certified for use in hazardous areas when used with the recommended power connection kits.		 ¡¡ CENELEC accepted approvals for Zone 1 and 2 heaters with T3 temperature limits are available.

The high temperature, Self Regulating XTV Tracer:
1. Withstands 420¢µ(215¡É) intermittent blowdown temperatures.
2. Can maintain temperatures up to 250¢µ(121¡É).

The low temperatures Self Regulating BTV Tracer:
1. Withstand up to 185¢µ(85¡É) blowdown temperatures.
2. Can maintain temperatures up to 150¢µ(65¡É).

The choice between XTV and BTV must be made based on the desired performance and the conditions of the application. XTV and BTV are backed by a 10 year performance guarantee.
