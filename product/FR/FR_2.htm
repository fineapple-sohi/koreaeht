M.I Fire Resistance Wiring Cable Survival Test


1. M.I Fire Resistance Cable

Alloy 825 sheath, mineral insulated electric cables for critical circuits such as emergency block valves, emergency lighting, and other monitoring equipment essential to a safe and orderly shutdown due to a fire.

　
Critical Circuit Survival
Operation During A Fire
Increased awareness and concern over loss of capital equipment and production time has prompted the development of equipment designed to deal with the destructive force of fire Much of this equipment required reliable source of electrical power to function. When exposed to high fire risk areas above grade electrical wiring is placed in jeopardy. Special precaution must be taken to assure electrical power is available when it is needed the most during a fire.


Emergency Block Valves (EBV's)
When the shutdown system of a refinery does not function properly a small fire can rapidly escalate to one of catastrophic proportion.
Conventional wiring methods survive for only a matter of minutes at temperatures well below those likely to be encountered in a hydrocarbon flash fire. In fact conduit and wire fails in less than 3 minutes in the 230 ~ 260 ℃ range. Temperature in excess of 1100℃ can be reached in a typical hydrocarbon flash fire.
To assure that the integrity of power and control wiring to emergency block valves is maintained during a fire special precautions must be taken Pyrotenax  cable systems offer the most reliable and least expensive method of assuring electrical function during a fire. Since plant standards are written with consideration given to the refinery and the products being produce two different cable types are available providing varied fire and  corrosion resistance.

　		 　 Fire Tested Electrical Cables
　 KoreaEHT cables have been subjected to an array of rigorous fire tests. Each test is different, providing a vital piece of information regarding the ability of the cable to survive various fire conditions.
　 　
　 　 　 　
　 Independent Rapid Temperature Rise Furnace Fire Test
　 This test, sponsored by a major oil company, subjected the Alloy 825 Cable to temperatures in excess of 1100℃, The test parameters required the cable to survive at 1100℃ for a period of 15 minutes. The 1100℃ temperature was reached within the first 9 minutes of the test. The test was conducted in an enclosed, insulated furnace fuel by propane gas. Throughout the entire test the cable was powering a 480 volt valve actuator.
　 　
　 　 　 　
　 Independent Hydrocarbon Flash Fire Test
　 Conducted by a major oil company, this test subjected Alloy 825 Cable to condition that closely approximate an actual hydrocarbon flash fire.
The testing procedure required that the cables be supported over a pit filled with a mixture of gasoline and diesel fuel. Upon ignition of the mixture, the temperature rise was both rapid and violent. During the testing the cables not only endured high temperatures, but also the destructive  turbulence created by the fire.
Throughout the test, the average temperature was in excess of 1100℃. Due to the nature of the test, however, there were temperature excursions both well above and well below this level.


2. 화재시 전력공급 케이블에 관한 성능 실험 (BS 6387 : 1994)

Performance Requirements for Cables required to Maintain Circuit Integrity under Fire Conditions
Resistance to Fire
----------------------------------------------------------
버너화염에 노출
Exposure to gas burner flames
Resistance to Fire with Water Spray
----------------------------------------------------------
650°C 화염에 15분간 노출시킨 후 물살과 화염에 15분간 노출
Exposure to flames at 650 °C for 15 minutes then exposure
to water spray and fire for 15 minutes
Resistance to Fire with Mechanical Shock
--------------------------------------------------------
철강판으로 30초 간격으로 15분간 충격을 주며 화염에 노출
Exposure to flames whilst mounting panel is struck
with a steel bar at 30 second intervals for 15 minutes
